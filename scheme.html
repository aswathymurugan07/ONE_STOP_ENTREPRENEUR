SCHEMES AND POLICIES





Few Available Schemes:


  • Venture Capitalist Scheme

    • Venture Capital Assistance is financial assistance provided by SFAC in the form of an interest-free loan to meet the capital shortfall of eligible projects to implement the project.

    Helping agribusiness entrepreneurs to invest in building agribusinesses through financial inclusion

    Provides financial assistance for preparation of Bankable/Bankable Project Report (DPRS) through Project Development Facility (PDF).

  • Single point Registration Scheme

    • The single largest buyer of a wide variety of goods is the government. The Government Store Purchases Program was launched in 1955-56 to increase purchases from the small-scale sector.

    NSIC registers Micro and Small Enterprises (MSME) under Single Point Registration Scheme (SPRS) to participate in government procurement.

  • Small Business Innovation Research Initiative (SBIRI)

    • The Small Business Innovation Research Initiative (SBIRI) of the Ministry of Science and Technology, Department of Biotechnology, was launched in 2005 to enhance public-private-partnership (PPP) efforts in the country.

    SBIRI was the first of its kind early-stage, innovation-focused PPP initiative in biotechnology. The launch of SBIRI has served as an enabling platform for the target organizations to realize their potential in developing their products and processes and promoting them in the market.

    Facilitates innovation and risk-taking by small and medium companies and brings together private industry, public institutions and the government under the same roof to promote research and innovation in the Indian biotech sector.




Central Government Schemes:


  • Pradhan Mantri Mudhra Yojana

    • To support the micro-enterprise sector MUDRA scheme provides loans up to ten lakhs without collaterals. Enterprises wanting to expand in fields of technology up-gradation or other projects can also take advantage of this scheme.

    Micro Units Development and Refinance Agency Limited. [Currency] A new development supported by NBFC in the micro enterprise sector of the country.

    Mudra helps various banks and micro-finance institutions by re-lending so that they are able to help other micro units by lending up to 10 lakh rupees as needed.

  • Women Enterpreneurship Schemes

    • Increasing presence of women as entrepreneurs has resulted in significant business and economic growth in the country.

    Women-owned business enterprises are playing an important role in society by creating employment opportunities in the country, changing demographics and inspiring the next generation of female founders.

State Government Schemes


  • Infrastructure Development Schemes

    • National Small Industries Corporation (NSIC) has launched this unique scheme to help startups establish their own offices and infrastructure.

    However, only those companies which fall under the official definition of startups, as highlighted by the Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises can avail this grant.

  • Credit Gurantee

    • Credit Guarantee Fund Trust for Micro and Small Enterprises (CGTMSE) has launched this unique Government scheme to help assist retailers, educational institutes, self-help groups, farmers and SMEs.

    Basically, the Credit Guarantee scheme has been launched to smoothen credit delivery system, as guarantee cover up to 85% is provided to the SMEs for loans up to Rs 5 lakh.
